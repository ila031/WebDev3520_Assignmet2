The Tale of Sita

I am Sita, the daughter of King Janaka of Mithila.

My life took a fateful turn when Lord Ram, a prince of Ayodhya, lifted the divine bow of Shiva at my Swayamvar and won my hand in marriage.

After some time I followed my husband Ram when he accepted 14 years of exile. Leaving the comforts of the palace behind, I joined him and his brother Lakshman in the forest, embracing a life of simplicity.

Then suddenly, I was abducted by Ravana, the demon King of Lanka. Though I was imprisoned in his palace, my heart with Ram.

When things were getting tough Hanuman, Ram's loyal devotee, found me. His courage and devotion brought me hope, and I knew Ram would come to rescue me.

A fierce battle took place as Ram, with the help of Hanuman and the Vanara army, fought Ravana. They were victorious!!

After Ravana's defeat, I was reunited with Ram. He gave me a test to walk on fire to prove my allegiance to him.

After that, we together returned to Ayodhya, where our people welcomed us with joy and reverence.

Our return marked the end of our exile, celebrated as Diwali, the festival of lights. It was a moment of triumph and a renewal of hope for our kingdom.

In the years that followed, Ram's reign, known as *Ram Rajya*, became a symbol of ideal governance. Together, we upheld the values of justice, compassion, and duty for the betterment of our people.

Later we had two sons, and I was exiled from Ayodhya by my husband Ram. I brought them up in an ashram, with the help of a sage.
