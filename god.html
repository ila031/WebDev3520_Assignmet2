The Tale of Lord Ram

I am Ram, the prince of Ayodhya. I am the eldest son of King Dasharatha and Queen Kaushalya. My life is a journey of love, sacrifice, and unwavering duty. However, at its core, it is a story of Sita. A woman who stood by me, even when times were dark.

I first saw Sita when she was stepping into a courtyard. The only way I could have her was by proving my strength as she was the princess of Mithila.

I picked up the divine bow of Lord Shiva at her Swayamvar, it was finalised, she was to be my wife. I took her home with me and we married each other.

However, shortly after due to a promise I made to my father, I accepted 14 years of exile in the forest. Sita and my brother Lakshman accompanied me on this journey.

When things were getting better and we were settling in Sita was abducted by the demon king Ravana.

With the help of Hanuman and an army of Vanaras, I declared a fierce battle to rescue her.

After defeating Ravana, I returned to Ayodhya with Sita and Lakshman, marking the end of my exile.

My return is usually celebrated as Diwali, which is the festival of lights.

Ram's reign, known as Ram Rajya, is remembered as a period of peace, prosperity, and justice.
